Community Government Infrastructure Project (CGIP)

Project Overview

The Community Government Infrastructure Project (CGIP) is designed to strengthen local governance and provide essential infrastructure across Bougainville. The project aims to empower community leaders and improve service delivery to meet local needs effectively.

Key Components

  • Community Halls: Construction of nine halls for local gatherings and events.
  • Government Offices: Establishment of ten offices to enhance administration and service delivery.
  • Solar Lighting Kits: Provision of over 7,000 solar kits to households to improve access to clean and affordable energy.

Capacity Building

  • Training Programs: Training for local leaders and community members to manage and maintain infrastructure.
  • Technical Support: Assistance to ensure sustainability and proper functioning of the projects.

Impact and Benefits

  • Enhanced Governance: Improved administrative capabilities and service delivery at the community level.
  • Community Empowerment: Increased participation of local leaders and members in decision-making.
  • Sustainable Development: Promoting renewable energy use and sustainable practices in local communities.

Project Partners

  • Australian Government: Funding and technical support for infrastructure and capacity building.
  • New Zealand Government: Support in provision of solar kits and other resources.
  • Autonomous Bougainville Government (ABG): Leading implementation and oversight at local level.
