Post-Referendum Strategic Support & Communications

Project Overview

Following the 2019 Bougainville Referendum, where over 97% of voters supported independence, the Post-Referendum Strategic Support & Communications project was launched. It supports transparent communication, inclusive consultations, and effective coordination between the National Government and the Autonomous Bougainville Government (ABG) to implement the Bougainville Peace Agreement.

Key Components

  • Institutional and Operational Support: Established a Joint Secretariat to coordinate the post-referendum process and provided technical and operational support to both governments.
  • Capacity Building: Training programs for government officials and stakeholders to enhance their ability to manage post-referendum processes.
  • Awareness and Communication: Media campaigns across TV, radio, print, and social media to inform the public.
  • Community Engagement: Outreach programs to ensure all segments of the population, including marginalized groups, are informed.
  • Inclusive Participation: Platforms for dialogue involving women, youth, veterans, and civil society organizations in decision-making.

Impact and Benefits

  • Enhanced Transparency: Improved information dissemination increased public trust in post-referendum processes.
  • Strengthened Governance: Capacity building enabled more effective governance and transition management.
  • Inclusive Decision-Making: Ensured that perspectives of all communities were considered.

Project Partners

  • UNDP: Provided technical and financial support.
  • Governments of Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, and the UK: Contributed funding and resources.
  • ABG: Led implementation at the local level.
  • National Government of PNG: Collaborated in planning and execution.
