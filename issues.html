"From Peace to Autonomy, to Referendum and Beyond"

Bougainville Peace Agreement

This section provides detailed information about the Bougainville Peace Agreement, its history, key components, and its significance in the journey towards peace and autonomy for the region.

The agreement, signed in 2001, marked the end of a decade-long conflict and established a framework for a political settlement, including provisions for weapons disposal, autonomy, and a referendum on independence.

Referendum

Here you can find information regarding the Bougainville referendum. This includes the process, the results, and the implications for the future of Bougainville.

The non-binding referendum was held in late 2019, with an overwhelming majority voting for independence from Papua New Guinea. This section explores the steps following this historic vote.

Consultation

This area is dedicated to the ongoing consultations between the Autonomous Bougainville Government (ABG) and the National Government of Papua New Guinea.

Following the referendum, a period of consultation is mandated to negotiate the political future of Bougainville. This section covers the progress, challenges, and key outcomes of these critical discussions.
